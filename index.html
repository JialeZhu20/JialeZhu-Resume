My Bachelor's Degree in Chemistry has afforded me experience in working with many different instrumentations and allowed me to use my knowledge to real world applications. Within the first few years at my time at the University of Califonia, Riverside, I have developed a great interest in the research of semiconductors, as it benefits my personal goal of working with solar cell technology.

Towards the end of my junior year spent at the University of California, Riverside, I joined a research lab and begun to work on synthesizing transition metal dichalcogenides, partically one called Molybdenum Disulfide. We probe these semiconductors for their characteristic and unique electronic and sprintronic properties By synthesizing these materials as a monolayer film product on a solid substrate, we can maximize the benefit obtained from the properties borne from the synthesis process.

Currently I am looking for an opportunity to further study and develop my knowledge and research in the semiconductor/solar technology field. A job within this field would be an option that I am completely open to. I am also exploring potential graduate school options as a secondary pathway.
